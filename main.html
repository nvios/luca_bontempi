Education

Ever since I was a kid, I always loved getting my hands dirty, either by building stuff or hacking around with my PC. By the time I became a teenager I was completely convinced that engineering would have been my future. My plans were clearly laid down in front of me, however, as motivational LinkedIn quotes from Elon Musk and Steve Jobs never cease to remind us, cross-contamination between different fields is a key success factor. Confident in that eye-opening consideration I decided to take a minor detour through the economics and statistics domain.

During my university years I got really passionate about inference and data science. I started playing around with MATLAB and later decided to step up my game following the "Python for machine learning ©" hype. For my final masters thesis I thought that it would have been fun for me to use what I learned to build an econometric model explaining the price variation of crypto-currencies and use machine learning to predict future cryptocurrency prices.

Experience

While studying for university, I worked part-time as a math and programming tuition teacher for freshmen as a mean for financing part of my student expenses. Things started getting a bit more serious with my first 9-5 job, working as a data and BI analyst for a consulting company. In my first weeks there I had a chance to familiarize with probabilistic modelling, SQL, a lot of messy data and, of course, a fair amount of overwhelmingly colorful spreadsheets and dashboards.

As I gained more experience and a better understanding of the job itself, I started to experiment with Python, KNIME and VBA to automate some of the processes. By the end of my experience, I contributed to the creation of a new integrated tool to streamline the data collection and analysis workflow and dynamic dashboard creation.

After concluding my Masters I joined Caffeina, a digital startup, as a part of the project management team. My first assignment consisted in reorganizing different data points, ranging from daily resources allocations to project expenditures and budgets, in order to be able to generate useful business insights. Once the different data sources where consolidated in a single dataset I swithched my focus to the creation of dynamic reports to provide a summary of the key metrics and indicators.

About one year after embarking in my experience with Caffeina, I was offered an opportunity to join the company that provided a soundtrack to my youth, Creative Technologies. Powered by a mix of excitement and existential doubts I booked a one way ticket to Singapore.

The Singaporean environment gave me an incredible exposure in terms of know-how and learning opportunities. In Creative I had a chance to work within the development team specializing in data science and analytics projects. My main project consisted in the design and deployment of a hybrid chatbot environment based on an NLP ensamble model. Besides the aforementioned, I also contributed to the creation of a marketplace analytics model to monitor the demand for different distribution channels (e.g. Amazon, Taobao, Creative.com and selected third party retailers) and minimize inventory and restocking costs.

Living in such a vibrant tech culture pushed me to step out of my comfort zone and be an active member of the data science community (more on my side-projects below). Due to the convenient location of their head offices and the great beer, the Google Dev community became my second home and a really challenging test bench. I recently started devoting good part of my time to study the different applications of ML and deep learning to the FinTech industry as part of CA-CIB's corporate branch.

Live Face Recogition using a webcam stream

Using the connected webcam the captured frames are processed by a Multi-task Cascaded Convolutional Network (MTCNN) that predicts the face location and Proposal Network (P-Net) is used to obtain the facial windows and their bounding box. All detected faces are fed to a different CNN, called Refine Network (R-Net), which reduces the number of false positives. After the preparation steps are completed, the framework uses Facenet to extract features to be used in the classification. The framework can detect also multiple people in a single frame.

Instagram Bot

For this project I created an Instagram bot to access a profile, gather information regarding followers and engagement and automatically like and follow people with similar interests and hashtags. The bot collects and stores information regarding liked pictures and followed profiles allowing to maximize the followed-to-followers ratio. Followed people that do not increase the engagement beyond a certain treshold are automatically unfollowed after a set time.

Invoice Classification and Sorting

The goal of the project was to automate the manipulation, classification and sorting of wholesale invoices. The featured script is part of an internal production application developed for the finance back office of one of the "tech big four". The present script is complemented by an NLP classifier extracting the key information for different invoicing and rebate documents. All the processed documents, after being classified, are saved to the correct location in a local database and processing logs are generated for debugging and analytics purposes.
